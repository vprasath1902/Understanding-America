    Checks and Balances

    The branches do not report to one another. Each branch has independent constitutional powers and ways to limit the others.

    Congress Checks

    Overrides vetoes, confirms appointments, controls spending, and can impeach.

    President Checks

    Vetoes bills, appoints judges, and executes laws.

    Courts Check

    Review laws and executive actions for constitutionality.

    How a Law Is Made

    An idea must pass through Congress, the President, and sometimes the courts before becoming durable national policy.

    Federalism

    American government is shared across federal, state, county, and local levels. This is why you have both a President and a Governor.

    Citizenship Pathway

    The naturalization process moves from green card eligibility to N-400, interview, oath, passport, and voting.
