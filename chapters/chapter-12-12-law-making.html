    Chapter 12

    How a Bill Becomes a Law

    A bill becomes law only after passing through several constitutional steps.

    Understanding is stronger than memorization. This chapter connects facts to meaning and real civic life.

    Learning Objectives

    • Define a bill
    • Trace committee, debate, vote, and presidential action
    • Understand veto and judicial review

    From Idea to Bill

    An idea may come from citizens, groups, agencies, the President, or members of Congress. Only members of Congress formally introduce bills.

    Committee Work

    Bills usually go to committees for hearings, study, changes, and recommendations. Many bills never leave committee.

    Both Chambers Must Agree

    The House and Senate must pass the same version. If versions differ, a conference committee may create a compromise.

    President and Courts

    The President may sign or veto. Congress can override a veto with two-thirds votes. Courts may later review whether a law is constitutional.
