    Chapter 15

    The Judicial Branch

    The courts interpret laws and protect the Constitution.

    Understanding is stronger than memorization. This chapter connects facts to meaning and real civic life.

    Learning Objectives

    • Describe the federal court system
    • Explain judicial review
    • Understand judicial independence

    Why Courts Exist

    When there is a legal dispute or constitutional challenge, courts provide an independent place to resolve it.

    Court Structure

    Federal cases may begin in District Courts, move to Courts of Appeals, and sometimes reach the Supreme Court.

    Judicial Review

    Judicial review allows courts to determine whether laws or government actions comply with the Constitution. Marbury v. Madison established this principle.

    Independence

    Federal judges are appointed by the President, confirmed by the Senate, and generally serve during good behavior so they can decide cases without election pressure.

    Remember ThisThe Supreme Court is the highest court.
