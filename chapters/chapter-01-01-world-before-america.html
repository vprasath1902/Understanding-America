    Chapter 1

    The World Before America

    Before there was a United States, North America was home to Indigenous nations and later European settlements that shaped the future country.

    Understanding is stronger than memorization. This chapter connects facts to meaning and real civic life.

    Learning Objectives

    • Recognize that the United States did not always exist
    • Understand why Europeans came to North America
    • Connect colonial self-government to later American democracy

    Before There Was a United States

    For thousands of years before European settlement, North America was home to many Indigenous peoples with distinct cultures, languages, economies, and systems of government. Nations such as the Cherokee, Haudenosaunee, Navajo, Powhatan, and many others lived across the continent long before the United States existed.

    Why Europeans Came

    European powers came for many reasons: land, trade, wealth, religious freedom, and national competition. Spain, France, England, and the Netherlands all sought influence in North America.

    England and the Atlantic Colonies

    England eventually established colonies along the Atlantic coast. These settlements developed local assemblies, courts, and traditions of self-government that later influenced the American constitutional system.

    Why This Matters

    The American Revolution did not begin in an empty land or an isolated moment. It grew from centuries of Indigenous history, European colonization, local self-government, and increasing conflict over authority.
