    Chapter 13

    The President of the United States

    The President leads the Executive Branch but does not rule the country.

    Understanding is stronger than memorization. This chapter connects facts to meaning and real civic life.

    Learning Objectives

    • Explain presidential powers
    • Understand limits on the President
    • Describe the Vice President and Cabinet

    Why the Presidency Exists

    The founders wanted energetic leadership but feared monarchy. The President enforces laws, represents the nation, and serves as Commander in Chief.

    Powers

    The President signs or vetoes bills, appoints officials with Senate confirmation, conducts foreign relations, grants federal pardons, and leads the Executive Branch.

    Limits

    The President cannot make laws alone, raise taxes, spend money without authorization, ignore the Constitution, or control Congress or the courts.

    Vice President and Cabinet

    The Vice President becomes President if needed and breaks Senate ties. The Cabinet advises the President and leads executive departments.
