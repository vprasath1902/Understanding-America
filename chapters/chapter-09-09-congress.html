    Chapter 9

    Congress

    Congress is the legislative branch and consists of the House of Representatives and the Senate.

    Understanding is stronger than memorization. This chapter connects facts to meaning and real civic life.

    Learning Objectives

    • Explain why Congress has two chambers
    • Describe congressional powers
    • Understand representation of people and states

    Article I Comes First

    The Constitution lists Congress first because lawmaking was expected to be a central responsibility of representative government.

    Two Chambers

    The House represents people by population. The Senate represents states equally. This was the Great Compromise.

    Powers of Congress

    Congress makes federal laws, levies taxes, spends money, declares war, oversees the Executive Branch, and has impeachment powers.

    Committees

    Much of Congress’s work happens in committees that study bills, hold hearings, and recommend changes.

    • Congress makes federal laws.
    • The House represents people; the Senate represents states.
    • Both chambers usually must agree before a bill becomes law.
