    Chapter 20

    Becoming an American Citizen

    Naturalization is the process through which eligible permanent residents become U.S. citizens.

    Understanding is stronger than memorization. This chapter connects facts to meaning and real civic life.

    Learning Objectives

    • Understand the N-400 process
    • Know what happens at the interview
    • Understand the oath and new responsibilities

    The Naturalization Path

    The process generally includes eligibility, Form N-400, biometrics, interview, English and civics testing, approval, and the oath ceremony.

    The Interview

    A USCIS officer reviews the N-400, confirms identity, asks eligibility questions, and administers the English and civics tests unless an exemption applies.

    The Oath

    You become a citizen after taking the Oath of Allegiance. The oath is a promise to support and defend the Constitution.

    After Citizenship

    New citizens can vote, apply for a U.S. passport, serve on juries when called, update Social Security records, and participate fully in civic life.
