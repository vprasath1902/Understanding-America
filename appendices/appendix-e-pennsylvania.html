    Pennsylvania Citizen’s Guide

    A practical reference for Pennsylvania residents learning how state and local government works.

    What This Appendix Covers

    • Pennsylvania state government
    • County, township, and school district responsibilities
    • Voting, driver licenses, state taxes, parks, and public services

    Pennsylvania Government

    Pennsylvania has its own Constitution and three branches of government: executive, legislative, and judicial. The Governor leads the executive branch, the General Assembly makes state laws, and Pennsylvania courts interpret and apply state law.

    General Assembly

    The Pennsylvania General Assembly has two chambers: the State Senate and the State House of Representatives. State legislators make laws on education, transportation, criminal justice, state taxes, and other Pennsylvania issues.

    Local Government

    For Chester Springs residents, local government may involve township supervisors, Chester County offices, and the Downingtown Area School District. Local officials handle many daily-life issues such as roads, zoning, parks, school budgets, elections administration, property records, and community planning.
