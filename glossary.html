    Interactive Glossary Starter

    A quick reference for key terms used throughout the book.

    Constitution

    The supreme law of the United States and the framework for the federal government.

    Federalism

    A system where power is shared between the federal government and state governments.

    Congress

    The legislative branch of the federal government, made up of the House and Senate.

    Senate

    The chamber of Congress where each state has two Senators.

    House of Representatives

    The chamber of Congress where representation is based on state population.

    Executive Branch

    The branch led by the President that enforces federal laws.

    Judicial Branch

    The branch that interprets laws and the Constitution.

    Bill of Rights

    The first ten amendments to the Constitution.

    Naturalization

    The process by which an eligible permanent resident becomes a U.S. citizen.

    Oath of Allegiance

    The final pledge taken before becoming a U.S. citizen.
