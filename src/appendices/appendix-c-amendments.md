The Constitution has been amended **27 times**. The first ten (the Bill of Rights, 1791) are covered in Appendix B; this appendix lists all twenty-seven. For official text, see [Constitution Annotated](https://constitution.congress.gov/).

| # | Year | Summary |

| 2 | 1791 | Right to keep and bear arms |
| 3 | 1791 | No forced quartering of soldiers |
| 4 | 1791 | No unreasonable search and seizure |
| 5 | 1791 | Due process; no self-incrimination or double jeopardy |
| 6 | 1791 | Right to a fair, speedy trial and a lawyer |
| 7 | 1791 | Jury trial in civil cases |
| 8 | 1791 | No excessive bail or cruel and unusual punishment |
| 9 | 1791 | Rights not listed are still retained by the people |

| 14 | 1868 | **Citizenship and equal protection** for all born in the U.S. |

## Patterns to Notice

Several amendments **expanded who can vote** (15th, 19th, 24th, 26th) and **expanded equality and citizenship** (13th, 14th, 15th). Others adjusted the machinery of government (12th, 17th, 20th, 22nd, 25th). Together they show a Constitution that endures by changing slowly and deliberately.
