The **Bill of Rights** is the first ten amendments to the Constitution. They were added in 1791 to place clear limits on government power and protect individual liberty. For the official text, see the [National Archives](https://www.archives.gov/founding-docs/bill-of-rights).

| Amendment | Protects |
| --- | --- |
| 1st | Freedom of **religion, speech, press, assembly, and petition** |
| 2nd | The right to keep and bear arms |
| 3rd | Freedom from being forced to quarter soldiers in your home |
| 4th | Protection from unreasonable **search and seizure** |
| 5th | **Due process**; protection from self-incrimination and double jeopardy |
| 6th | The right to a speedy, public trial and to a lawyer |
| 7th | The right to a jury trial in many civil cases |
| 8th | Protection from excessive bail and cruel and unusual punishment |
| 9th | People keep other rights not specifically listed |
| 10th | Powers not given to the federal government are **reserved to the states and the people** |

## Why It Matters

The First Amendment's five freedoms are the tools of a self-governing people, and the protections in the Fourth through Eighth Amendments ensure fair treatment by the legal system. The Ninth and Tenth Amendments make clear that the list of federal powers is limited and that rights extend beyond what is written down.
