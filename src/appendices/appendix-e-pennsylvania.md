The main book is written for a national audience. This appendix is a **state-specific guide**, using Pennsylvania as a worked example. It is also the natural home for the **state answers on the civics test** (your state capital, your governor, your U.S. senators, your representative). Other states can be added later by copying this format.

> For the people currently holding these offices, always check [Appendix H: Current Officials & Fees](current-officials.html) — names change, the structure does not.

## Pennsylvania Government

Pennsylvania has its own constitution and three branches of government, mirroring the federal design. The **Governor** leads the executive branch, the **General Assembly** makes state laws, and Pennsylvania's courts interpret and apply state law. The state capital is **Harrisburg**.

## The General Assembly

Pennsylvania's legislature, the **General Assembly**, has two chambers: the **State Senate** and the **State House of Representatives**. State legislators make laws on education, transportation, criminal justice, state taxes, and other Pennsylvania matters. You are represented by one state senator and one state representative based on where you live.

## Local Government

Below the state are layers of local government. Most Pennsylvanians live under a **county**, a **municipality** (a city, borough, or township), and a **school district**. Local officials — county commissioners, township supervisors or city council, and school board members — handle everyday matters such as roads, zoning, parks, school budgets, election administration, property records, and community planning.

## Common Services

| Need | Likely office |
| --- | --- |
| Driver license or REAL ID | PennDOT (PA Dept. of Transportation) |
| Voter registration | County election office / PA Department of State |
| State income tax | PA Department of Revenue |
| Property records | Your county |
| Local zoning or roads | Your municipality (city, borough, or township) |
