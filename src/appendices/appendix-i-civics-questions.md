These are the **128 official civics questions** for the **2025 version** of the test. The USCIS officer asks up to **20**, and you must answer at least **12** correctly to pass. A **★** marks the **20 questions** available under the **65/20 special consideration** (applicants 65+ who have been permanent residents for 20+ years study only the starred questions and may test in their chosen language).

Each question ends with a **→ chapter link** showing where in this book to read more. Some answers change with elections and appointments — for those, see **[Appendix H: Current Officials & Fees](current-officials.html)** and confirm at **[uscis.gov/citizenship/testupdates](https://www.uscis.gov/citizenship/testupdates)**. Applicants on the older **2008 (100-question)** test should use the [USCIS Citizenship Resource Center](https://www.uscis.gov/citizenship).

# American Government

## A: Principles of American Government

**1. What is the form of government of the United States?** — Republic; Constitution-based federal republic; representative democracy. → [Ch 6](../chapters/chapter-06-06-constitution.html)

**2. What is the supreme law of the land?** ★ — The (U.S.) Constitution. → [Ch 6](../chapters/chapter-06-06-constitution.html)

**3. Name one thing the U.S. Constitution does.** — Forms the government; defines the government's powers; defines the parts of government; protects the rights of the people. → [Ch 6](../chapters/chapter-06-06-constitution.html)

**4. The Constitution starts with the words "We the People." What does "We the People" mean?** — Self-government; popular sovereignty; consent of the governed; people should govern themselves; (example of) social contract. → [Ch 6](../chapters/chapter-06-06-constitution.html)

**5. How are changes made to the U.S. Constitution?** — Amendments; the amendment process. → [Ch 6](../chapters/chapter-06-06-constitution.html)

**6. What does the Bill of Rights protect?** — The basic rights of Americans / of people living in the United States. → [Ch 7](../chapters/chapter-07-07-bill-of-rights.html)

**7. How many amendments does the U.S. Constitution have?** ★ — Twenty-seven (27). → [Ch 6](../chapters/chapter-06-06-constitution.html)

**8. Why is the Declaration of Independence important?** — It says America is free from British control; all people are created equal; it identifies inherent rights and individual freedoms. → [Ch 3](../chapters/chapter-03-03-road-to-independence.html)

**9. What founding document said the American colonies were free from Britain?** — The Declaration of Independence. → [Ch 3](../chapters/chapter-03-03-road-to-independence.html)

**10. Name two important ideas from the Declaration of Independence and the Constitution.** — Equality; liberty; social contract; natural rights; limited government; self-government. → [Ch 6](../chapters/chapter-06-06-constitution.html)

**11. The words "Life, Liberty, and the pursuit of Happiness" are in what founding document?** — The Declaration of Independence. → [Ch 3](../chapters/chapter-03-03-road-to-independence.html)

**12. What is the economic system of the United States?** ★ — Capitalism; free market economy. → [Ch 36](../chapters/chapter-36-36-american-economy.html)

**13. What is the rule of law?** — Everyone must follow the law; leaders, government, and everyone must obey it; no one is above the law. → [Ch 13](../chapters/chapter-13-13-separation-checks.html)

**14. Many documents influenced the U.S. Constitution. Name one.** — Declaration of Independence; Articles of Confederation; Federalist Papers; Anti-Federalist Papers; Virginia Declaration of Rights; Fundamental Orders of Connecticut; Mayflower Compact; Iroquois Great Law of Peace. → [Ch 6](../chapters/chapter-06-06-constitution.html)

**15. There are three branches of government. Why?** — So one part does not become too powerful; checks and balances; separation of powers. → [Ch 13](../chapters/chapter-13-13-separation-checks.html)

## C: Rights and Responsibilities

**63. There are four amendments about who can vote. Describe one.** — Citizens 18+ can vote; no poll tax; any citizen (men and women) can vote; a male citizen of any race can vote. → [Ch 25](../chapters/chapter-25-25-elections.html)

**64. Who can vote in federal elections, run for federal office, and serve on a jury?** — Citizens (U.S. citizens). → [Ch 26](../chapters/chapter-26-26-citizenship.html)

**65. What are three rights of everyone living in the United States?** — Freedom of expression, speech, assembly, to petition, of religion; the right to bear arms. → [Ch 7](../chapters/chapter-07-07-bill-of-rights.html)

**66. What do we show loyalty to when we say the Pledge of Allegiance?** ★ — The United States; the flag. → [Ch 26](../chapters/chapter-26-26-citizenship.html)

**67. Name two promises new citizens make in the Oath of Allegiance.** — Give up loyalty to other countries; defend the Constitution; obey U.S. laws; serve the nation/military if needed; be loyal to the U.S. → [Ch 26](../chapters/chapter-26-26-citizenship.html)

**68. How can people become U.S. citizens?** — Be born in the U.S. (14th Amendment); naturalize; derive citizenship. → [Ch 26](../chapters/chapter-26-26-citizenship.html)

**69. What are two examples of civic participation?** — Vote; run for office; join a party or civic group; contact officials; support an issue; write to a newspaper. → [Ch 26](../chapters/chapter-26-26-citizenship.html)

**70. What is one way Americans can serve their country?** — Vote; pay taxes; obey the law; serve in the military; run for or work for government. → [Ch 26](../chapters/chapter-26-26-citizenship.html)

**71. Why is it important to pay federal taxes?** — Required by law and by the Constitution (16th Amendment); funds the federal government; civic duty. → [Ch 36](../chapters/chapter-36-36-american-economy.html)

**72. Why must men age 18–25 register for the Selective Service?** — Required by law; civic duty; makes the draft fair if ever needed. → [Ch 26](../chapters/chapter-26-26-citizenship.html)
