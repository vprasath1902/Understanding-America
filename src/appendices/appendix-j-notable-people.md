Several civics questions ask you to name a famous American and say why they are important. This appendix gathers **every person named in the 128 questions** — the historical figures the test expects you to recognize, plus the **current officeholders** for this edition. Each entry says **why the person matters** and links to the chapter that tells the fuller story.

For the offices that change with elections and appointments (President, Vice President, Speaker, Chief Justice, your governor, your members of Congress), the names below are current for this edition and are kept up to date in **[Appendix H: Current Officials & Fees](current-officials.html)**. Always confirm the latest at **[uscis.gov/citizenship/testupdates](https://www.uscis.gov/citizenship/testupdates)**.

# Founders and the Revolutionary Era

**George Washington** — Commander of the Continental Army in the Revolution and the **first President of the United States**. Known as the **"Father of Our Country,"** he set the example of a president who serves and then steps down. → [Ch 4](../chapters/chapter-04-04-american-revolution.html)

**Thomas Jefferson** — Principal author of the **Declaration of Independence (1776)**, whose words "all men are created equal" and "Life, Liberty, and the pursuit of Happiness" defined the nation's founding ideals. Later the third President. → [Ch 3](../chapters/chapter-03-03-road-to-independence.html)

**Benjamin Franklin** — A leading Founder: **U.S. diplomat** who won French support in the Revolution, the **first Postmaster General**, and the oldest delegate at the Constitutional Convention. He also started one of the first free public libraries. → [Ch 6](../chapters/chapter-06-06-constitution.html)

**James Madison** — Called the **"Father of the Constitution"** for shaping its framework, he was also one of the three authors of the **Federalist Papers** and the fourth President. → [Ch 6](../chapters/chapter-06-06-constitution.html)

**Alexander Hamilton** — The **first Secretary of the Treasury**, who built the young nation's financial system, and a co-author of the **Federalist Papers** arguing for ratifying the Constitution. → [Ch 6](../chapters/chapter-06-06-constitution.html)

**John Jay** — A co-author of the **Federalist Papers** (with Madison and Hamilton) and the first Chief Justice of the United States. → [Ch 6](../chapters/chapter-06-06-constitution.html)

# The Civil War and Abolition

**Abraham Lincoln** — The **16th President**, who led the country through the **Civil War**, **preserved the Union**, and issued the **Emancipation Proclamation** that freed enslaved people in the Confederate states. → [Ch 9](../chapters/chapter-09-09-civil-war-reconstruction.html)

# Reform and Rights Movements

**Susan B. Anthony** — A leader of the **women's rights movement** in the 1800s who campaigned for women's right to vote, helping lay the groundwork for the 19th Amendment (1920). Other leaders of that movement include **Elizabeth Cady Stanton, Sojourner Truth, Harriet Tubman,** and **Lucretia Mott**. → [Ch 8](../chapters/chapter-08-08-growing-nation.html) · [Ch 10](../chapters/chapter-10-10-industrial-immigration.html)

**Martin Luther King, Jr.** — The best-known leader of the **civil rights movement**, who fought to **end racial discrimination** and win equal rights through nonviolent protest. → [Ch 12](../chapters/chapter-12-12-cold-war-civil-rights.html)

# The Twentieth Century

**Franklin D. Roosevelt (FDR)** — President during **both the Great Depression and World War II**, the longest-serving President in U.S. history. → [Ch 11](../chapters/chapter-11-11-depression-world-wars.html)

**Dwight D. Eisenhower** — A commanding **general in World War II** before becoming the **34th President**. → [Ch 11](../chapters/chapter-11-11-depression-world-wars.html)
