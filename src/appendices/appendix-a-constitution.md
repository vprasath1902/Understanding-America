The Constitution is short — a preamble, seven articles, and twenty-seven amendments. This guide summarizes how it is organized. For the complete official text, see the [National Archives transcript](https://www.archives.gov/founding-docs/constitution-transcript).

## The Preamble

The Constitution opens by stating its purposes: to "form a more perfect Union, establish Justice, insure domestic Tranquility, provide for the common defence, promote the general Welfare, and secure the Blessings of Liberty." Its first three words — **"We the People"** — establish that the government's authority comes from the people.

## The Seven Articles

| Article | What it does |
| --- | --- |
| I | Creates **Congress** (the legislative branch) and lists its powers |
| II | Creates the **Presidency** (the executive branch) |
| III | Creates the **Supreme Court** and federal courts (the judicial branch) |
| IV | Governs relationships among the **states** |
| V | Explains how to **amend** the Constitution |
| VI | Makes the Constitution the **supreme law** (Supremacy Clause) |
| VII | Set the rules for **ratifying** the Constitution |

## How the Constitution Changes

Article V allows the Constitution to be **amended**. An amendment must be proposed (usually by a two-thirds vote of both houses of Congress) and then ratified by three-fourths of the states. This deliberately difficult process has produced just 27 amendments in more than 230 years.
