## The First World War

When World War I broke out in Europe, the United States at first stayed out. In 1917 it entered on the side of the Allies, and fresh American troops and supplies helped bring the war to an end in 1918. President Woodrow Wilson led the country during the war and hoped to build a lasting peace, though the United States ultimately chose not to join the new League of Nations.

## The Great Depression

The prosperity of the 1920s ended in 1929 when the stock market crashed, beginning the Great Depression — the worst economic crisis in the nation's history. Banks failed, businesses closed, and millions lost their jobs and homes. President Franklin D. Roosevelt responded with the **New Deal**, a sweeping set of programs to provide relief, create jobs, and reform the economy. Lasting institutions such as Social Security date from this era, and the federal government's role in everyday life grew significantly.

## The Second World War

The Depression was still easing when World War II engulfed the globe. On December 7, 1941, Japan attacked the U.S. naval base at Pearl Harbor, and the United States entered the war alongside the Allies against the Axis powers. American industry and armed forces played a decisive role on multiple fronts, and the war ended in 1945. It also brought hard chapters at home, including the internment of Japanese Americans.

## A Global Power

The United States emerged from the war as one of the two strongest nations on Earth. Its economy was powerful, its military had global reach, and it took a leading role in building new international institutions such as the United Nations. The decisions made in these years set the stage for the next half-century.
