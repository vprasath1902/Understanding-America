## The States’ Voice

Every state has two Senators regardless of population. This protects smaller states and balances the House.

## Six-Year Terms

Senators serve six-year terms, and only about one-third are elected every two years. This creates stability and continuity.

## Unique Powers

The Senate confirms major presidential appointments, approves treaties, and conducts impeachment trials.

## Direct Election

Originally state legislatures chose Senators. The Seventeenth Amendment created direct election by voters.
