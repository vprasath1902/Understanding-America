## Who Can Vote

In federal elections, voters generally must be U.S. citizens, meet age and residency requirements, and register if their state requires it. Green Card holders cannot vote in federal elections.

## Many Offices

Voters choose Presidents, Senators, Representatives, Governors, state legislators, county officials, township supervisors, school boards, and judges where applicable.

## Primaries and General Elections

Primaries choose party nominees. General elections choose the officeholder from nominees and other candidates.

## Electoral College

The President is elected through the Electoral College. A state’s electoral votes equal its Senators plus Representatives.
