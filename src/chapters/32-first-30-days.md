## Protect Your Certificate

The **Certificate of Naturalization** is your proof of citizenship, and it is difficult and costly to replace (Form N-565). Store the original somewhere safe, make a few copies for your records, and avoid carrying the original in your wallet day to day. Treat it like your most important document — because, along with a passport, it is.

## Register to Vote

One of the first things many new citizens do is **register to vote** — a right that was not available to them before. Registration rules vary by state, and you can begin at **vote.gov**, which links to every state's system. Registering soon means you will be ready for the next election, whether it is national, state, or local.

## Apply for a Passport and Update Records

A **U.S. passport** lets you travel internationally and serves as convenient proof of citizenship; you can apply using your Certificate of Naturalization through the U.S. Department of State. It is also wise to update your records with the **Social Security Administration** to reflect your new citizenship, and to update your employer or other institutions if needed.

## Begin Civic Life

Citizenship is the start of a lifelong relationship with your community and country. Learn who your representatives are at each level, follow elections and issues, answer the call for **jury duty**, attend local meetings, and take part respectfully in public life. The chapter *Government Around Me* is a good guide to getting started close to home.
