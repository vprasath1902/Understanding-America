## Fear of Another King

After fighting Britain, Americans feared concentrated power. The Articles of Confederation gave most authority to the states and created a very weak national government.

## Weaknesses of the Articles

The national government could not tax effectively, regulate interstate commerce, maintain a strong national army, or resolve state disputes easily. There was no President and no Supreme Court.

## Shays’ Rebellion

The rebellion in Massachusetts alarmed leaders such as George Washington and James Madison. It suggested the national government might be too weak to maintain order.

## The Philadelphia Convention

Delegates met in 1787 to revise the Articles but decided to write an entirely new Constitution. The central question was how to create a government strong enough to govern but limited enough to protect liberty.
