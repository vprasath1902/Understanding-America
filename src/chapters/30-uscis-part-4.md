## Modern History

After World War II came the **Cold War**, a long rivalry with the Soviet Union; the main U.S. concern during this time was the spread of **communism**. At home, the **Civil Rights Movement** worked to **end racial discrimination** and segregation. **Martin Luther King Jr.** led much of the movement through nonviolent protest. More recently, the terrorist attacks of **September 11, 2001** reshaped American security and foreign policy.

## Geography

A few geography facts cover most of the test. The **Atlantic Ocean** is on the **East Coast** and the **Pacific Ocean** is on the **West Coast**. **Canada** is the country to the north and **Mexico** is to the south. The **capital** of the United States is **Washington, D.C.**, and the Statue of Liberty stands in New York Harbor.

## National Symbols

The American **flag** has **13 stripes** representing the original thirteen colonies and **50 stars** representing the fifty states. The **national anthem** is **The Star-Spangled Banner**. The **Statue of Liberty** — a gift from France — has long welcomed immigrants and symbolizes freedom. These symbols carry the history covered earlier in the book.

## Holidays

The most important national holiday is **Independence Day** on **July 4**, marking the adoption of the Declaration of Independence. Other federal holidays include New Year's Day, Martin Luther King Jr. Day, Memorial Day, Juneteenth, Labor Day, Veterans Day, Thanksgiving, and Christmas. Knowing a few of these is enough for the civics test, and celebrating them is part of shared civic life.
