## A War Few Expected America to Win

Britain had a professional army, powerful navy, money, and global reach. The colonies had limited funds, weak central authority, and a small army, but they had determination and home-field advantage.

## George Washington’s Leadership

Washington understood that he did not need to conquer Britain; he needed to keep the Continental Army alive. His endurance and integrity made him a national symbol.

## Turning Points

Saratoga convinced France that the Americans could win. French military and naval support became decisive. Valley Forge tested the army, while Yorktown trapped Cornwallis and effectively ended major fighting.

## Independence Recognized

The Treaty of Paris in 1783 recognized the independence of the United States. The new country now faced the challenge of governing itself.
