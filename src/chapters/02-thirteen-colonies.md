## Three Colonial Regions

Historians often group the colonies into New England, Middle, and Southern regions. Geography, climate, religion, and economy shaped each region differently.

## New England Colonies

Massachusetts, New Hampshire, Rhode Island, and Connecticut had rocky soil, cold winters, fishing, shipbuilding, trade, and strong town-meeting traditions.

## Middle Colonies

New York, New Jersey, Pennsylvania, and Delaware had fertile farmland, rivers, trade, and religious diversity. Pennsylvania, founded by William Penn, became known for religious tolerance.

## Southern Colonies

Maryland, Virginia, North Carolina, South Carolina, and Georgia developed agricultural economies with crops such as tobacco, rice, and indigo. Enslaved Africans were forced to provide much of the labor in many areas.
